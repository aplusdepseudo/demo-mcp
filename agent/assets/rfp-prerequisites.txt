ENTERPRISE RFP PREREQUISITES AND COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS
=========================================================
Version: 2.0 | Classification: Internal Use Only
Last Updated: 2026

This document defines the mandatory prerequisites, evaluation criteria, and compliance
requirements that must be addressed in all enterprise procurement RFPs. Procurement teams
must verify that every RFP covers all applicable sections before issuing to vendors.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. VENDOR QUALIFICATION CRITERIA
------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.1  The vendor must have been operating as a registered business entity for a minimum of
     5 years at the time of proposal submission.
1.2  Annual revenue must exceed $10 million USD (or equivalent) for each of the last 3
     fiscal years. Audited financial statements are required.
1.3  The vendor must not appear on any government debarment, exclusion, or suspension list
     (EU, US SAM.gov, or applicable national registry).
1.4  A minimum of 3 verifiable reference customers with comparable scope (industry, size,
     solution complexity) must be provided.
1.5  Key project personnel (project manager, solution architect, lead developer) must have
     a minimum of 5 years of relevant experience and must be named in the proposal.
1.6  Subcontractors contributing more than 20% of work must meet the same qualification
     criteria and be explicitly listed in the proposal.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. SECURITY AND COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1  The vendor must hold an active ISO 27001:2022 certification covering the services
     delivered under this RFP.
2.2  A SOC 2 Type II report (less than 12 months old) must be provided or made available
     under NDA.
2.3  Full GDPR (or applicable regional privacy regulation) compliance must be demonstrated,
     including data processing agreements.
2.4  Data residency: all data must remain within the agreed geographic region (EU or North
     America) unless explicitly approved in writing.
2.5  The vendor must provide a documented vulnerability management and patch policy with
     SLAs for critical, high, and medium severity vulnerabilities.
2.6  Penetration testing must be conducted at least annually; results summary must be
     available upon request.
2.7  Multi-factor authentication (MFA) must be enforced for all administrative access to
     systems in scope.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. FINANCIAL AND CONTRACTUAL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1  Pricing model: fixed-price or capped time-and-materials (T&M). Open-ended T&M is
     not acceptable for the base scope.
3.2  Payment terms: Net 30 days from invoice receipt and acceptance.
3.3  Warranty period: minimum 12 months post go-live covering defect remediation at no
     additional cost.
3.4  Service Level Agreement (SLA): production system availability must be 99.5% or higher
     per calendar month, excluding pre-approved maintenance windows.
3.5  Incident response SLAs: Critical (P1) – 1 hour response, 4 hours resolution target;
     High (P2) – 4 hours response, 24 hours resolution target.
3.6  Liability cap: the vendor's maximum aggregate liability must be at least equal to the
     total contract value.
3.7  Intellectual property: the client retains ownership of all custom code, configurations,
     and documentation developed specifically for this engagement.
3.8  Right to audit: the client reserves the right to audit vendor systems and processes
     related to this engagement with 10 business days' notice.
3.9  Change management: a formal change control process must be proposed, including impact
     assessment templates and approval workflows.
3.10 Exit/transition plan: a documented transition and data export plan must be included,
     covering all migration artifacts, credentials, and documentation hand-off.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1  The proposed solution must be cloud-native or validated as cloud-ready, deployable
     on Microsoft Azure or AWS (specify in proposal).
4.2  APIs must be RESTful, versioned, fully documented (OpenAPI 3.0 spec required), and
     include sandbox/test environment access.
4.3  The solution must support integration with the enterprise Microsoft 365 and Azure AD
     stack (SSO via SAML 2.0 / OIDC is mandatory).
4.4  End-user interfaces must be mobile-responsive (WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standard).
4.5  Multi-language support (minimum English and French) and multi-currency (minimum USD,
     EUR, GBP, CAD) are required.
4.6  Disaster Recovery (DR): RTO ≤ 4 hours, RPO ≤ 1 hour for production data.
4.7  The solution must support role-based access control (RBAC) with a minimum of 5
     configurable roles.
4.8  Data encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+) is mandatory.
4.9  Audit logging must capture all user actions with timestamps, user IDs, and be
     tamper-proof and retained for a minimum of 3 years.
4.10 The vendor must provide a documented software development lifecycle (SDLC) and
     release management process, including hotfix procedures.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. DELIVERY AND PROJECT MANAGEMENT
------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1  Agile delivery methodology (Scrum or SAFe) is required; sprint cadence must be
     2 weeks.
5.2  A dedicated, named project manager (PMP or equivalent certified) must be assigned
     for the duration of the project.
5.3  Weekly status reports and bi-weekly steering committee meetings are mandatory.
5.4  A detailed project plan (WBS) with milestones and critical path must be included
     in the proposal.
5.5  A comprehensive testing strategy (unit, integration, UAT, performance, security)
     must be proposed.
5.6  A training program for end users, power users, and administrators must be included
     in scope with at least 8 hours of training material.
5.7  Hypercare support period of minimum 4 weeks post go-live must be included.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. SUSTAINABILITY AND ESG
------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1  The vendor must have a published Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) or ESG policy
     and report (last 12 months).
6.2  Carbon footprint reduction initiatives or a net-zero/carbon neutral roadmap is
     strongly preferred and will be positively weighted in evaluation.
6.3  A diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) policy covering the vendor's workforce
     practices must be provided.
6.4  The vendor must comply with applicable labor laws in all countries of operation and
     must not use forced or child labor in its supply chain (Modern Slavery Act compliance).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. EVALUATION CRITERIA WEIGHTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Category                          | Weight
----------------------------------|-------
Technical Fit                     | 30%
Vendor Qualification & References | 20%
Commercial / Pricing              | 20%
Project Approach & Timeline       | 15%
Security & Compliance             | 10%
ESG / Sustainability               | 5%

------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. MANDATORY DELIVERABLES IN PROPOSAL
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Vendors must include the following in their response:
  - Executive summary (max 2 pages)
  - Company profile and financial summary
  - Proposed solution architecture diagram
  - Requirements compliance matrix (against this document)
  - Detailed project plan and timeline
  - Staffing plan with CVs for key personnel
  - Pricing breakdown (by phase and category)
  - References (min. 3, with contact information)
  - Security and compliance certifications
  - Sample SLA agreement
  - ESG policy summary
